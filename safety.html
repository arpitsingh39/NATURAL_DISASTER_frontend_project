    Earthquake

  • Do's
    • Keep away from old, tall or detached buildings, electricity wires, slopes and walls, which are liable to collapse.
    • Stop the vehicle away from buildings, walls, slopes, electricity wires and cables
    • Leave your badly damaged house.
    • Collect water containers, food items and ordinary and special medicines (for persons with heart complaints, diabetes etc.)
  • Dont's
    • Don’t rush to the doors or exits, never use the lift.
    • Keep away from broken windows, mirrors, chimneys and furniture.
    • Avoid places where there are loose electric wires and do not touch any metal object in contact with them.
    • Don’t re-enter badly damaged buildings and don’t go near damaged structures.

    Volcanic Eruption

  • Do's
    • Evacuate immediately if lava flow, lahars, or pyroclastic flows are heading toward you, and follow evacuation orders from authorities without delay.
    • Stay indoors during ashfall, keeping all windows, doors, and ventilation closed to prevent ash and gases from entering your home.
    • Wear protective gear such as N95 masks, goggles, and long clothing to protect your respiratory system, eyes, and skin from volcanic ash if you must go outside.
    • Keep emergency supplies ready, including food, water, medications, and communication devices, and have a plan for pets and livestock.
  • Dont's
    • Don't drive in heavy volcanic ash, as it can clog engines, reduce visibility, and make roads slippery and dangerous.
    • Don't use air conditioning, fans, or heaters during ashfall as they can draw ash and toxic gases indoors.
    • Don't go near rivers or low-lying areas following an eruption, as lahars or mudflows may occur unexpectedly.
    • Don't stay in or return to restricted or evacuation zones until authorities declare them safe.

    Flood

  • Do's
    • Tune to your local radio for warnings and advice.
    • Move vehicles, equipments, garbage, chemicals, oldpersons, children, pregnant women etc. to higher and safe places.
    • Disconnect all electrical appliances.
    • Turn off electricity and gas before you leave the house.
  • Dont's
    • Don/t allow children to ply in, or near, flood waters.
    • Never wander around in flooded area.
    • Don’t drive into water of unknown depth and current.
    • Don’t eat food, which is affected by floodwater.

    Wildfire

  • Do's
    • Do close all windows and doors to prevent smoke from entering your home.
    • Do keep a damp cloth handy to cover your nose and mouth if you are exposed to smoke.
    • Do have an evacuation kit ready with essentials like water, food, medicine, and a flashlight.
    • Do stay low to the ground to avoid smoke inhalation if caught in a smoky area outdoors.
  • Dont's
    • Don't use candles, fireplaces, grills, or other open flames indoors during wildfire conditions to prevent accidental fires.
    • Don't return to your home until officials say it's safe due to hidden hazards like hot ash and unstable structures.
    • Don't inhale wildfire smoke; always wear an N95 mask or cover your face with a damp cloth if masks are unavailable.
    • Don't block roads or firefighting routes; keep vehicles away from wildfire access paths.

    Drought

  • Do's
    • Do conserve water rigorously by fixing leaks, taking short showers, using buckets instead of running water, and only running dishwashers or washing machines with full loads.
    • Do practice rainwater harvesting and repair or rejuvenate local water bodies before the rainy season to help replenish groundwater.
    • Do use drought-resistant plants for landscaping and use efficient irrigation methods like drip or sprinkler systems to minimize water use.
    • Do stay informed through local news, radio, or official sources for drought updates, water restrictions, and safety instructions.
  • Dont's
    • Don't waste water by letting taps run unnecessarily, flushing toilets when not needed, or using flowing water for chores like washing utensils.
    • Don't cut trees or forests which help maintain soil moisture and prevent erosion.
    • Don't interfere with traditional water sources like ponds, wells, and tanks, and avoid over-extraction of groundwater to preserve these vital supplies.

    Storm

  • Do's
    • Do stay indoors during a storm and seek shelter in a sturdy building or a hard-top vehicle with windows closed.
    • Do unplug electrical appliances and avoid using corded phones or electrical equipment to prevent electric shock from power surges.
    • Do stay away from windows, doors, and porches to avoid injury from flying debris and broken glass.
    • Do prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, flashlight, batteries, and first aid supplies before the storm hits.
  • Dont's
    • Don't take shelter under isolated trees or tall structures that attract lightning.
    • Don't use plumbing or water during a thunderstorm as lightning can travel through pipes and water.
    • Don't stay outside or in open areas like fields, parks, or near bodies of water during a storm.
    • Don't drive through flooded roads or attempt to pass through water-covered areas during or after heavy rain and storms.
